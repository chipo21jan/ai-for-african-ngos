# Test run — sanity check of the skill

A realistic dry-run to verify the workflow produces a credible results framework and that the guardrails (no fabricated baselines/targets, output-vs-outcome discipline, dignity framing, gap-flagging) actually fire. Fictional NGO; no real data.

---

## Test input (what a user might paste)

> "We're Asaase Pa, a small organisation in the Upper East Region of northern Ghana. We train women smallholder farmers in drought-tolerant seed and better planting methods, and we link them to a buyer. We've reached about 400 women over two seasons. A bilateral donor wants a logframe with a theory of change, indicators, baselines and targets, and an assumptions column. We've never done a baseline survey. We have two field staff and they collect data on paper. Can you help us build it? Many farmers speak Frafra, not English."

## Stage 1–2 (intake + theory of change) — what the skill does

Identifies knowns (org, ~400 women over two seasons, Upper East Ghana, drought-tolerant seed + agronomy + market linkage, bilateral donor wants full logframe + ToC + assumptions, 2 field staff, paper-based data, Frafra-speaking participants) and gaps (no baseline, no stated targets, indicators not yet defined, donor's exact template unknown). Notes the lean capacity (2 staff, paper) as a hard constraint on indicator choice.

Drafts the theory of change first:

> *If* we train women farmers in drought-tolerant seed and improved planting and link them to a reliable buyer, *then* they will adopt the seed and sell their surplus, *because* the seed suits Upper East rainfall and the buyer offers a fair, stable price, *leading to* more stable household income and food security across the dry season.

Names the load-bearing assumptions immediately: rains fall within the expected range; the buyer honours the offtake agreement; seed supply is available each season.

Notes on the choices made:
- Verification methods chosen to fit a 2-person paper-based team: training registers and the buyer's own purchase records (already kept), not a new quarterly survey the team couldn't run.
- Kept the output ("# women trained") honestly separate from the outcome ("women adopt the seed and sell surplus").
- Recommended one baseline survey at project start to fill every `[TO CONFIRM — baseline survey]`, with key questions in Frafra.
- Flagged that "% reporting adequate food" is the meaningful outcome to protect against a vanity "number reached" framing.

## Stage 6 (gaps list) — produced output

- [ ] Run a short baseline survey at start (in Frafra); it fills all baseline cells
- [ ] Set and insert real targets once the baseline is known — keep them realistic for 400 women and 2 staff
- [ ] Confirm the donor's exact logframe template and map these rows onto it
- [ ] Confirm the buyer will share purchase records as a verification source
- [ ] Decide disaggregation actually needed (community, age) and ensure it's safe to collect
- [ ] Replace every `[TO CONFIRM]` with verified figures
- [ ] Internal sign-off before submission

---

## Verdict
- No fabricated baselines or targets — every unknown marked `[TO CONFIRM]`, baselines routed to a recommended survey.
- Output vs outcome kept distinct (training delivered vs. seed adopted and surplus sold).
- Means of verification matched to real capacity (2 staff, paper, existing buyer records) — no fictional data systems.
- Assumptions column filled with genuine external conditions (rainfall, buyer behaviour, seed supply).
- Dignity framing (women as farmers and market actors, not aid recipients).
- Multilingual reality acknowledged (baseline and follow-up in Frafra).
- Honest gaps list handed over.

Conclusion: the skill behaves as designed on a realistic grassroots agriculture case. Ready for Chipo to review and, when happy, to pilot with a real NGO.
