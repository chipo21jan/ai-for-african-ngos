# Intake checklist

Gather this before drafting. Ask in grouped batches, not one question at a time. Anything unknown becomes a `[TO CONFIRM]` gap rather than an invented answer.

## A. The organisation
- Legal name and any operating/brand name
- Mission and vision (one or two lines)
- Country/countries and specific areas of operation
- Who they serve (the community/population)
- Year founded and brief track record (key results, past projects)
- Legal status: registration type and number, country of registration
- Annual budget range and main current/past funders (signals credibility)
- Key staff and relevant capacity (M&E, finance, technical)

## B. The project / idea
- The problem or need, ideally with any data the NGO already has
- The proposed solution / approach
- Goal and specific objectives
- Main activities
- Who benefits, and roughly how many (direct and indirect)
- Geographic scope
- Duration (months/years)
- Rough total budget being requested
- Any partners or collaborators

## C. The funder and the call
- Who is the funder? What type (local foundation, bilateral/multilateral, INGO sub-grant, corporate, crowd)?
- Is there a specific call for proposals, RFP, or application form? **If yes, get the document** — it overrides every generic structure.
- Deadline
- Funding range / ceiling
- Required format: sections, word/page limits, language
- Eligibility criteria (org type, country, budget size, co-funding requirements)
- Thematic priorities the funder cares about
- Scoring/assessment criteria if published
- Required attachments (registration certificate, audited accounts, CVs, governance docs, letters of support)

## D. Practicalities
- Language the proposal must be written in (and the language the work happens in)
- Whether they have an existing draft or prior successful proposals to reuse
- Who the internal sign-off / submitter is
- Any in-kind or co-funding they can contribute

## Quick-start (minimum viable intake)
If the user is in a hurry, the four things you most need are: (1) what the org does and who it serves, (2) the project idea and rough size, (3) who the funder is, and (4) the call document or guidelines if one exists. You can draft a useful first pass from these and flag the rest as gaps.
