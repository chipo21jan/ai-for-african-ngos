## 1. Executive summary

## 2. Organisational background
Mission, area of operation, who you serve, track record and relevant past results, legal/registration status `[TO CONFIRM registration no.]`, and the capacity that makes you credible to deliver this.

## 3. Problem statement / needs
The problem, grounded in evidence (data, lived experience, credible sources). Who is affected and how. Why it matters now. `[TO CONFIRM data sources]`

## 4. Project description
**Goal:** the long-term change you contribute to.
**Objectives:** specific, measurable aims.
**Activities:** what you'll actually do, in plain terms.
**Beneficiaries:** who benefits, how many (direct/indirect) `[TO CONFIRM numbers]`, and how they were involved in shaping this.

## 5. Results framework
A short results chain (outputs → outcomes → impact) with indicators, baselines `[TO CONFIRM baselines]`, targets, and means of verification. Add a one-line theory of change.

## 6. Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning
How you'll track progress, collect data, and learn. Who is responsible. How you'll report to the funder.

## 7. Work plan / timeline
Activities mapped across the project period (a simple table or Gantt-style list).

## 8. Budget summary
Budget by category, tied to activities. M&E funded. Overhead within the funder's cap. `[TO CONFIRM all figures]` Attach a detailed line-item budget if required.

Team, governance, systems (finance, M&E), and any partners with their roles.
