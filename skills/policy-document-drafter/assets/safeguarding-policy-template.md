## 1. Purpose
[ORG NAME] is committed to keeping children and at-risk adults safe from harm in all of its work. This policy sets out that commitment and the steps everyone connected to the organisation must follow.

## 2. Scope
This policy applies to all staff, volunteers, board members, contractors, consultants and anyone acting on behalf of [ORG NAME] `[TO CONFIRM the full list]`. It applies in all of the organisation's activities and locations.

## 3. Definitions
For this policy, a **child** is a person under the age of `[TO CONFIRM the legal age under local law]`. An **at-risk adult** is `[TO CONFIRM definition for the jurisdiction]`. The forms of harm this policy addresses include physical abuse, emotional/psychological abuse, sexual abuse and exploitation, neglect, and `[adapt to local legal definitions — TO CONFIRM]`.

## 4. Policy statement
[ORG NAME] believes that the safety, wishes and dignity of every child and at-risk adult come first. We will not tolerate abuse or harm by anyone connected to our work, and we commit to preventing it, responding to it promptly, and putting the person at risk — not the reputation of the organisation — at the centre of our response.

## 5. Code of conduct
Everyone acting for [ORG NAME] will: treat children and at-risk adults with respect; follow this policy and report concerns. No one may: `[list prohibited behaviours, e.g.]` be alone with a child without justification; have any sexual contact with a beneficiary; use physical punishment; or share images or information about a child without proper consent `[adapt to the org's real activities — TO CONFIRM]`.

## 6. Safe recruitment and vetting
[ORG NAME] will reduce risk through safe recruitment: making safeguarding expectations clear in adverts, asking about it at interview, checking references, and carrying out background/criminal-record checks where these are lawfully available `[TO CONFIRM what vetting is available locally]`. We will only commit to checks we can actually carry out.

## 7. Reporting a concern
Anyone — a child, parent, community member, staff member or volunteer — can raise a safeguarding concern. Concerns can be raised with the Designated Safeguarding Lead (see section 9). If a concern is about that person, it can instead be raised with `[TO CONFIRM an alternative route, e.g. the board chair]`. No one who raises a concern in good faith will face any disadvantage. `[TO CONFIRM: include accessible routes for children and for staff working in other languages.]`

## 8. Response and referral
Our first priority is the immediate safety of the person at risk. On receiving a concern, [ORG NAME] will: take steps to keep the person safe, assess the concern, and refer it to the appropriate external authority — `[TO CONFIRM the correct child-protection authority / police and the mandatory-reporting duty for the jurisdiction]` — where required or where the person is at risk. Records will be kept securely and shared only on a need-to-know basis, in line with `[TO CONFIRM local data-protection law, e.g. POPIA / Kenya DPA / Nigeria NDPA]`.

## 9. Designated Safeguarding Lead
The Designated Safeguarding Lead is `[TO CONFIRM position]`, with `[TO CONFIRM deputy position]` as deputy. They are the point of contact for concerns and are responsible for managing referrals, keeping records and reviewing this policy.

## 10. Training and awareness
All staff and volunteers will be made aware of this policy on induction and trained to recognise and report concerns, with refresher training at least `[TO CONFIRM frequency]`. The board will also be briefed on it.

## 11. Survivor- and child-centred principles
Throughout any safeguarding response, [ORG NAME] will listen to the child or survivor, take them seriously, never blame them, keep them informed appropriately, and offer or signpost support. Their safety and dignity guide every decision.
